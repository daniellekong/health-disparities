Socioeconomic Disparities in Health Outcomes

Healthcare access and health outcomes are greatly fractured along socioeconomic and racial lines in the United States. The numerous articles produced by popular publishers and public health researchers underscore the severity of this issue and the lack of solutions in place to address it.

The simple data visualization-analysis presented here explores this health disparity issue, using diabetes prevalence as an indicator of health outcome and average income as an indicator of socioeconomic status. The data used is at the state-level, and thus nuances of geography and other variables are not captured. The aim of this visualization is to convey a relationship between health outcome and socioeconomic status.

30.3 million Americans have diabetes
Individuals with diabetes spend approximately $9,600 per year on medical costs associated with diabetes
Diabetes is a top 10 leading cause of death in the United States
